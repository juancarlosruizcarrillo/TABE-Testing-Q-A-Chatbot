Frequently Asked Questions for TABE Testing

What do my continuing students do on the first day if they don’t have to test?

Face-to-Face Classes: Continuing students who do not need to test should not attend class on the first day. Teachers will receive a list of students currently enrolled, with those highlighted in blue indicating they do not need to test. Please inform these students that their first day of class will be after testing day. The first day of classes is reserved primarily for testing.

Online Classes: Teachers will receive a list of students, with those highlighted in blue indicating they do not need to test. Coordinate with the assessment team to schedule testing for those who need progress tests.

What do I do if a student says they have to test but they don’t have a test ticket?

Verify the student’s testing status by:

  • Checking the testing list report sent to you.
  • Confirming whether the student is listed as requiring testing (those not needing to test are highlighted in blue).
  • If the student is not on your roster but claims they registered, contact the registration office immediately at adult.education@centralaz.edu or 520-494-5952.
What if a student’s log-in doesn’t work?

1. Ensure the student is entering the correct credentials.

2. For issues with the DRC INSIGHT application, contact juan.ruiz@centralaz.edu or the Instructional Data Coach for support.

3. For CAC campus computer logins, refer to the “ghost logins” list provided in your email. If these do not work, visit the learning center for alternative logins.

What do I do if a student accidentally starts the wrong test?

Pause the test immediately and notify the assessment coordinator.

1. Document the incident to ensure proper reporting according to policy.

2. Follow procedures to reset the test or assign the correct assessment.

What happens if a student doesn’t finish their test on the first day?

1. Tests should typically be completed within the scheduled three-hour class time. Ensure students are guided through instructions to avoid delays.

2. If a test is incomplete, inform the assessment coordinator to schedule a follow-up session.

3. While practice tests are optional and can reduce testing time, subject tests that are started should be completed in one session.

4. For Reading & Language tests, sessions may be split across days, but they must be completed within the first 12 hours of attendance to establish an accurate Educational Functioning Level (EFL).

How long is each test?

ESL Students: Listening and Reading tests typically take 1–1.5 hours per section.

GED Students:

  • Reading: ~1.5 hours
  • Language: ~1 hour
  • Mathematics: ~1 hour

TABE Complete Battery: ~2.5 hours

Visit the TABE 11&12 Maximum Testing Times for more details.

What do I do if a student becomes upset or overwhelmed? And what do I do if I catch them cheating?

If a student becomes upset or overwhelmed:

Pause the test, allow the student to take a break, and offer reassurance. Refer them to support services or reschedule their test if necessary. Document the situation following program guidelines.

If you catch a student cheating:

Immediately stop the test, confiscate unauthorized materials, and document the incident. Inform the assessment coordinator to determine next steps, which may include invalidating the test or requiring a retake.

Unauthorized Materials Prohibited During Testing:

  • Electronic devices (phones, watches, etc.)
  • Books, papers, pens, mechanical pencils, or highlighters
  • Food, drink, backpacks, or loose clothing
  • Any device capable of recording or reproducing test content

Testing Irregularities to Report:

  • Unauthorized materials
  • System interruptions or technical issues
  • Disturbances or disruptive examinees
  • Examinee illness or refusal to leave after dismissal
